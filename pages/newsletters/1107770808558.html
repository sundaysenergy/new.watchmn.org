BOARD BRIEF   

WATCH in the news    

Last week's brief included a monitor note about a woman wanting her husband to be able to come home, even though he had strangled her, because she needs his income to feed her children. We have been receiving a lot of feedback on the letter to the editor on that subject that Marna had published in the Star Tribune. Volunteers, justice system members, and directors of advocacy programs have thanked her for addressing how poverty and DV conspire to trap battered women, especially those with children.  

Introducing people to WATCH   

It's easy! That's the message I've heard from people who attended the luncheon at new board member Mary Grace Flannery's home last Thursday. Ten people attended, including Denny Scharlemann and Jennifer Johnson, who, along with Mary Grace, talked about their volunteer experiences and why they support WATCH and our mission.  The women who attended were very interested and inspired by the conversation, and asked thoughtful questions about our work.

We will follow up with those who attended to thank them, invite them to Gala, and invite them to make a gift to WATCH. Thanks, Mary Grace, for hosting this fun event. Any of you who have been too nervous to consider something similar should talk to Mary Grace, Jennifer or Marna. It's educational, inspiring, and effective. You can do it, too!  
WATCH Volunteer in the spotlight     
On Monday, several of us attended a reading at Magers and Quinn booksellers by WATCH volunteer Russ Van Heel. Russ read from his first book, Life in Purgatory: A Book of Short Stories. Russ began volunteering with WATCH after leaving an abusive relationship and credits his volunteer experience here with helping him regain confidence, find a new job, and move forward with his life. We hope you will join us in recognizing him with the Volunteer of the Year award next Wednesday, September 28 at 5 pm at Kate Kelly's home, 2322 West Lake of the Isles Parkway, Minneapolis.
