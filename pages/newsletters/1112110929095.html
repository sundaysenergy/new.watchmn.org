The Clothesline Project

The Minnesota Coalition For Battered Women is looking for volunteers to make t-shirts for their Clothesline Project. If you would like to get involved, visit their website here or email Safia Lovett.

The Clothesline Project started in October 1990 in Massachusetts as part of an annual Take Back the Night March. Today, there are Clothesline Projects in nearly every state and in at least five countries.

The Clothesline Project honors both survivors and victims of intimate partner violence by allowing those affected to tell their stories using artwork or words to decorate a shirt, and then displaying those shirts--hanging on a clothesline--as a method of raising awareness of the war against women.  Learn more about the history behind the Clothesline Project here.
