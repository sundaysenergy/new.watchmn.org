You Won't Want to Miss This
WATCH is thrilled to be hosting a presentation by
The Honorable Elizabeth Cutter
in our office on Thursday, April 11th from 11:30-1:00.
Judge Cutter recently transitioned to the bench after working for years as a prosecuting attorney. As the senior assistant county attorney responsible for felony domestic prosecution, Cutter worked many of the cases monitored by WATCH. She has also played a major role in the Gun Surrender Committee, and shares WATCH's goal of getting guns out of the hands of domestic abusers.
Don't miss the opportunity to hear Judge Cutter talk about her experience from two different vantage points.

Your Place in History
"Hold those things that tell your history and protect them." --Maya Angelou

It started as International Women's Day in 1911, in 1980 it grew to Women's History Week, and in 1987 Congress declared March as Women's History Month in perpetuity. Generations of women have shaped our history, and reflecting on the stories that have brought us to this point not only shows us how far we've come, but how far we still have to go.

This March is a special one for WATCH because it was in March 1993 that monitors first entered the Hennepin County courts. What started twenty years ago as a group of devoted volunteers has grown into an organization that is a national and international leader in court monitoring. In WATCH's twenty year history, over 950 volunteers have monitored cases of violence against women and children to make our present courts more effective than our past. You, our 106 presently active volunteers, ensure the future will look even better.

Take a moment this March of 2013 to reflect on the history you are a part of at WATCH: how far we've come and where you think we could go in the next twenty.
